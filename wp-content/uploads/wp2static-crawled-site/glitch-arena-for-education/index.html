Glitch Arena for Education

Glitch Arena is a simple, child-safe multiplayer FPS. It has been designed for accessibility, non-violence and inclusivity. It can be played offline in pairs or teams, and can also be used as a base for interdepartmental, cross-disciplinary school projects.

Why Glitch Arena? It’s a PG version of a quintessential genre, popularised by Quake – the first esports title. Arena FPS is fast paced but highly strategic, and Glitch represents the best of it. Including AFPS in your esports rotation completes it! 

A Child-safe FPS

Glitch is a rare example of a fast-paced competitive style first person shooter (FPS) that is also inoffensive and non-violent. 

Non-violent

Potentially violent words such as “kill” are replaced with alternatives such as “decommissioned.” Common weapon names are presented as slang: shotgun becomes shotty, railgun becomes rails, and nail gun becomes nailer.

An intent from the outset was to remove the gratuity common to the FPS genre. It demonstrates that fun can be had without the usual associated gore. The goal is to keep competition friendly and de-personalise the experience.  

Non-human characters

Importantly, the characters in the game are comically non-human, resembling a rolling droid. 

High School Esports

As high schools expand their e-sports curriculum, policy around these activities is playing catch up. Glitch Arena offers a safe way to grow basic FPS skills, engage in healthy team building, and compete. 

Offline Access

Glitch Arena can be played without internet access, and does not rely on third party services such as Steam. This is useful in schools with internet usage policies that prevent online access.

Lightweight

Esports are typically cost prohibitive for all but the better funded schools because of the computing power required. Glitch Arena was built to be playable on the most basic of modern windows computers. Because it was made to run on Intel HD 5000 series IGPUS from 2013, It will play on basically any computer since 2014.This makes the game available to more schools and more students.

Cross-Curriculum Creativity

As well as being a playable game, Glitch Arena is available as an Unreal Engine “blueprint only” project, meaning it can be used as a base to modify or create content. Schools can use Glitch Arena as the canvas for cross-disciplinary inter-faculty projects:

IT

The game is developed in Unreal Engine blueprints which is a visual coding language great for learning the logical underpinning of traditional line-based code. 

Digital Design

Game design relies on a strong user experience foundation in the game levels, interface and HUD. Students can create in-game levels and test them together as they go.

Visual Art

Glitch Arena has a strong 90s aesthetic, but runs on the modern Unreal Engine. Students can create any type or size of levels for Glitch Arena, as well as experiment with Glitch-style pixel-art materials. 

Music

Video game music is a great introduction to commercial music production. Students can also replace the game’s sound effects with their own creations. 

Accessibility

We are continually improving the means of access to Glitch Arena. To date we have implemented various community requested features: 

  • Customisable colours for opponents, crosshair, 
  • Large, easy to see characters and items
  • Customisable crosshair size
  • HUD (heads up display) sizing options
  • Visual and audible cues for important in-game events
  • De-emphasis on hard to acquire mechanical skills

Work continues on compatibility with alternative modes of input for accessibility devices. 

Glitch Arena is decidedly simple. With a smaller number of variables a player needs to track, the cognitive load can be shifted to the meta game. It is fast and fun, but emphasises strategy and teamwork. 

Inclusivity

In addition to removing physical barriers to play, Glitch Arena avoids content that may be offensive in any culture, religion or ethnicity. It rejects the ‘edgy’ culture that is prevalent in online multiplayer games, and seeks to promote goodwill.
